Les & Pam

ARE GETTING

MARRIED!

September 14th, 2013

Les

I remember when Pam and I met like it was yesterday. I was at a point in my life where I was floundering. I wasn't unhappy. I was just uncertain. My life was grey, desaturated, and drifting. Pam and I had spoken before, but never at length. One day, she wandered into my office and asked to join me for lunch. We started talking and it happened. We clicked. Over the next few weeks, we kept talking. It was like someone was splashing my grey world with technicolor. I couldn't get enough. Before I knew it, I was in love; and the world was a vivid, colorful, aromatic place. Looking back, that day changed my life.

In the following 10 years, we have worked together, played together, and raised fur friends together. In that time together, I have learned so much from her and about her. She is wise, intelligent, beautiful, and tenacious. She is a woman who values integrity, family, and friends. She is loving, patient, and kind. Most of all, she 'gets' a computer geek like me. She is, in a word, my best friend.

On New Year's eve last year, she said YES; and my world changed again. Now as I look forward to our life together, I dream in color. I see a journey soaked in the same bold, vibrant color that she splashed me with all those years ago. To quote Louis Armstrong:

I see trees of green….red roses too
I see em bloom….for me and you
and I think to myself what a wonderful world.
I see skies of blue…. clouds of white
Bright blessed days…dark sacred nights
And I think to myself…what a wonderful world.

She said YES!

Pam

After traveling for years with my job, I needed to let myself catch up personally. So, I decided to stay put in Charlotte for awhile. I interviewed for 2 potential jobs. There was no way I could know that the one I choose would change my life forever. That’s right, I met my future husband at that job some 10 years ago. Myself, Les, and a few other IT contractors were in a building all by ourselves. One day, we were both swamped with work. So, we decided not to join the rest of the team and stay in for lunch. I asked Les if I could bring my lunch over to his desk to take a break while I ate. We immediately clicked. The conversation flowed as if we had known each other all of our lives. We began talking on the phone for hours at night. Soon, we went out on a date. 10 years later, and 4 adopted dogs (our children), he asked me to Marry him. I couldn’t be happier. I'm so glad that I found someone to spend my life with. While we have so much in common, it's our differences that make every day interesting. He accepts me for who I am and always encourages me to be a better person.

I can’t wait for this next journey in my life!

Thank you all for sharing this great moment in our life!! We are so blessed to have all
